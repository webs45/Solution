Smart Study - Classification of Plants

Q.3 Answer the following questions in your own words.
  1. Write the characteristics of subkingdom Phanerogams.
  2. Distinguish between monocots and dicots.
  3. Write a paragraph in your own words about the ornamental plants called ferns.
  4. Sketch, label and describe the Spirogyra.
  5. Write the characteristics of the plants belonging to division Bryophyta.
Answer:

a. Characteristics of subkingdom Phanerogams:

  • Seed-bearing plants with well-differentiated body (roots, stem, leaves).
  • Vascular system present (Xylem & Phloem).
  • Distinct reproductive organs; produce flowers, cones, or fruits.
  • Divided into Gymnosperms and Angiosperms.

b. Distinguish between monocots and dicots:

CharacteristicMonocotsDicots
SeedOne cotyledonTwo cotyledons
Root SystemFibrousTaproot
StemVascular bundles scatteredVascular bundles in a ring
Leaf VenationParallelReticulate
Flower PartsMultiples of 3Multiples of 4 or 5

c. Paragraph about ferns:

Ferns (Pteridophyta) are ornamental plants with lush, divided leaves (fronds). They reproduce via spores, not seeds, and thrive in shaded, moist areas. Common types include Boston fern, Maidenhair fern, and Staghorn fern.

d. Sketch, label and describe Spirogyra:

Description: Spirogyra is filamentous green algae with unbranched cylindrical cells. Spiral chloroplasts are distinct. Reproduces by fragmentation (asexual) and conjugation (sexual).

Note: Draw a sketch showing filamentous structure, cell wall, nucleus, and spiral chloroplast. This will help visualize the organism.

e. Characteristics of Bryophyta:

  • Amphibians of plants; need water for reproduction.
  • Soft, ribbon-like body (thallus); no true roots, stems, leaves.
  • Rhizoids present for anchorage; lack vascular tissues.
  • Examples: Moss (Funaria), Liverworts (Marchantia).
