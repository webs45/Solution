Q.2 Complete the following statements with suitable terms and explain:

1. Thallophyta plants have soft and fibre-like body.

Explanation: Plants like Algae have soft, filamentous thallus bodies without true roots, stems, or leaves.

2. Bryophyta is called the 'amphibian' of the plant kingdom.

Explanation: Bryophytes like Moss require water for sexual reproduction but grow on land, hence 'amphibians' of plants.

3. In pteridophytes, asexual reproduction occurs by spore formation and sexual reproduction occurs by zygote formation.

Explanation: Ferns reproduce asexually via spores and sexually via fusion of gametes to form zygote.

4. Male and female flowers of gymnosperms are borne on different sporophylls of the same plant.

Explanation: Gymnosperms like Cycas bear male and female reproductive organs separately on the same plant.
